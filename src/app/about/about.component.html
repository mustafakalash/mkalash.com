About Me

My name is Mustafa Kalash and I am passionate about software, education, engineering... and fun

My Story

I am a first-generation American, the son of a Syrian immigrant. Growing up stuck between two cultures has taught me a lot about empathy and the benefits of diversity. One thing I've always loved to do is solve problems, so at a young age, I found joy in learning programming, starting with web development and Java. In high school, I joined a competition engineering team which competed in trebuchets, robotics, and the All-American Soapbox Derby. This club would set me on the path to where I am now. Through it, I learned design, manufacturing, mechanics, and C++. But I also grew through teamwork and adversity. With that team I found a home, but for longer than I could have thought.

At The Ohio State University, I began my undergraduate studies, majoring in Computer Science and Engineering. Meanwhile, I continued to work with the team as an Assistant Coach. I also started teaching for a non-profit called TECH CORPS, which sought to bring technology education to underprivileged students. Through these opportunities, I discovered my love for education, leading to a change in major to Career-Technical Education, which I earned my degree in after 3 years. Soon after, I began teaching at Pickerington High School Central, the same place I attended. My subjects include computer science, cybersecurity, engineering, construction, robotics, and other technology courses. There, I also became head coach of the club, and lead the team through many accomplishments, including world championships. While there, I completed my Master's in Classroom Technology with a concentration in computer science from Bowling Green State University. Eventually, I emerged as a leader, and became the head of my department, which is where I am today.
